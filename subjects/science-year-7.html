üî¨ Biology:

"What are the differences between plant and animal cells?"

üí°

Plant cells have 3 extra parts:

Cell wall - rigid structure that gives shape and support (made of cellulose)
Chloroplasts - green organelles that capture sunlight for photosynthesis
Large permanent vacuole - stores cell sap and helps maintain cell structure

Both have: nucleus, cytoplasm, cell membrane, mitochondria, ribosomes

These differences allow plants to make their own food and stand upright without a skeleton!

üß™ Chemistry:

"How can I tell if something is a pure substance or a mixture?"

üí°

Pure substances:
‚Ä¢ Have exact, fixed melting and boiling points
‚Ä¢ Made of only ONE type of particle
‚Ä¢ Cannot be separated by physical methods
‚Ä¢ Examples: pure water, pure gold, pure oxygen

Mixtures:
‚Ä¢ Have ranges of melting/boiling points
‚Ä¢ Made of TWO or MORE different particles mixed together
‚Ä¢ CAN be separated by physical methods (filtering, distillation, chromatography)
‚Ä¢ Examples: salt water, air, muddy water

‚ö° Physics:

"What's the difference between speed and velocity?"

üí°

Speed is how fast something moves - it's just a number with units like m/s or mph. For example, "the car is travelling at 30 mph".

Velocity is speed AND direction together. It tells you both how fast AND which way something is moving. For example, "the car is travelling at 30 mph north". If you change direction but keep the same speed, your velocity changes but your speed stays the same!

üî¨ Biology:

"What are the seven life processes all living things do?"

üí°

MRS GREN is the memory trick for the seven life processes:

Movement - changing position or moving parts
Respiration - releasing energy from food
Sensitivity - detecting and responding to surroundings
Growth - increasing in size or developing
Reproduction - producing offspring
Excretion - removing waste products
Nutrition - taking in nutrients for energy and growth

ALL living things must do ALL seven processes!

üß™ Chemistry:

"What happens when we mix an acid with an alkali?"

üí°

Neutralisation reaction happens:

Acid + Alkali ‚Üí Salt + Water

Example: Hydrochloric acid + Sodium hydroxide ‚Üí Sodium chloride + Water

The acid cancels out the alkali, and the alkali cancels out the acid. The pH moves towards 7 (neutral). This is why we take antacid tablets for stomach acid - the alkali in the tablet neutralises the excess acid in our stomach!

‚ö° Physics:

"Can energy be created or destroyed?"

üí°

NO! This is the Law of Conservation of Energy.

Energy can ONLY be:
‚Ä¢ Transferred from one place to another
‚Ä¢ Transformed from one type to another

Examples:
‚Ä¢ Electrical energy ‚Üí Light energy + Heat energy (in a bulb)
‚Ä¢ Chemical energy ‚Üí Kinetic energy + Heat energy (in muscles)
‚Ä¢ Gravitational potential energy ‚Üí Kinetic energy (falling object)

The total amount of energy in the universe NEVER changes!
