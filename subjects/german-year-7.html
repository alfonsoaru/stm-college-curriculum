Frequently Asked Questions

What will my child learn in their first term of German?

+

In the first term, students begin with "Meine Welt und ich" (My World and Me), learning to introduce themselves, talk about their age, name, and where they live. They'll master numbers 1-19, basic question words, and start using the key verbs 'haben' (to have) and 'sein' (to be). The second half of the term focuses on family and pets, expanding vocabulary to describe family members, pets, physical descriptions, and important dates like birthdays.

How does the German curriculum connect to real life?

+

Our German curriculum is built around practical themes that students can relate to. They learn to talk about sports they play, describe their school subjects and teachers, plan holidays, and go shopping in German-speaking countries. Each module includes authentic materials and real-world scenarios, preparing students for genuine communication in German-speaking environments.

What grammar will students master by the end of Year 7?

+

By the end of Year 7, students will have mastered present tense forms of essential verbs (haben, sein, spielen), ordinal and cardinal numbers, basic adjectives for descriptions, opinion expressions using 'gern', reason-giving with 'weil', prepositions (in, an, auf, neben), and future expressions using the present tense. They'll also be comfortable with 'es gibt' constructions and 'ich m√∂chte' for expressing wants.

How are students assessed in Year 7 German?

+

Assessment is balanced across all four language skills. Each module includes a formal assessment: Module 1 focuses on reading comprehension, Module 2 on listening skills, Module 3 returns to reading, Module 4 emphasizes writing for communication, and Module 5 tests listening again. The year concludes with comprehensive end-of-year exams covering listening, reading, and writing, while speaking is assessed continuously throughout the year.

What resources and support are available for German learning?

+

Students use a comprehensive pupil book with integrated activities and regular "Lernzieltest" (learning objective tests) and "Wiederholung" (revision) sections at the end of each module (pages 20-21, 42-43, 66-67, 88-89, and 110-111). The curriculum includes built-in retrieval practice through "Do now" tasks and scheme-of-work activities, plus assessment review sessions to reinforce learning.
