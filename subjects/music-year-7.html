Music Year 7 Q&A

What musical skills will students develop in Year 7?
Students develop vocal performance through choral work, keyboard skills with two-handed playing, rhythm composition using Samba conventions, and digital composition using Sibelius software. They learn to read music notation and understand musical structure.
How is music theory integrated into practical work?
Theory is taught through practical application including solfege and Kodaly methods for vocal work, treble clef reading for keyboard, rhythmic notation for Samba, and structural understanding through composition projects across different musical styles.
What technology is used in music education?
Students use Sibelius composition software for creating original compositions across various genres including pop, classical concerto, and contemporary minimalist styles. This develops both creative and technical skills in digital music production.
How are students assessed in Music?
Assessment includes individual vocal and keyboard performances, small group practical work on percussion, and composed submissions using digital software. Students demonstrate both technical proficiency and creative understanding through varied musical activities.
