Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What makes STM's Design & Technology curriculum unique?
A: Our curriculum combines traditional making skills with cutting-edge technology including CAD/CAM systems and Photoshop. Students experience both Product Design and Food Technology, developing creative problem-solving skills while understanding the complete design process from conception through to manufacture and evaluation.
Q: What equipment and materials do students need?
A: All specialist tools, equipment, and materials are provided in lessons. Students should have a basic pencil case with pencils, ruler, and rubber. For Food Technology lessons, students need to bring ingredients as specified on ingredient sheets provided in advance.
Q: How does Year 7 prepare students for GCSE Design & Technology?
A: Year 7 introduces fundamental concepts that underpin GCSE study including the ACCESSFMM annotation framework, technical drawing skills, material understanding, and the iterative design process. Students develop independence, analytical skills, and practical competence essential for higher-level study.
Q: What is the balance between theory and practical work?
A: Our curriculum emphasizes hands-on making with approximately 70% practical work and 30% design theory. Students learn theoretical concepts through practical application, ensuring deep understanding of materials, processes, and design principles through direct experience.
Q: How is Food Technology integrated into the curriculum?
A: Food Technology runs alongside Product Design throughout the year. Students learn nutrition science, food safety, and the Eat Well Guide while developing practical cooking skills. Each practical session builds culinary techniques from basic knife skills through to complex dish assembly and presentation.

  • "What is the difference between CAD and CAM?"
    Answer: CAD (Computer-Aided Design) is using computer software like Photoshop to create and modify designs digitally. CAM (Computer-Aided Manufacture) is using computer-controlled machines like laser cutters to make products from those designs. CAD helps you design it, CAM helps you make it!
  • "Why do we consider Fair Trade when choosing materials?"
    Answer: Fair Trade ensures that workers who produce materials are paid fairly and work in safe conditions. As designers, we have a responsibility to consider the social and environmental impact of our material choices, not just cost and appearance. Ethical design creates positive change in the world.
  • "What are the key differences between natural and synthetic fabrics?"
    Answer: Natural fabrics (cotton, wool, silk) come from plants or animals and are biodegradable but can shrink or wrinkle. Synthetic fabrics (polyester, nylon) are made from chemicals and are strong, wrinkle-resistant, but don't breathe as well. Each has advantages depending on the product's purpose.
  • "How do I thread a sewing machine correctly?"
    Answer: Start with the spool on top, thread through guides from back to front, down through the tension discs, through the take-up lever, down through the needle guides, and finally through the needle eye from front to back. The bobbin thread comes up through the needle plate to create stitches together.
  • "What is applique and how is it used decoratively?"
    Answer: Applique is attaching fabric shapes onto a background fabric to create decorative patterns or pictures. The fabric pieces can be sewn by hand using running stitch or backstitch, or by machine. It adds texture, color, and visual interest to textile products.
  • "What safety rules must I follow when using a sewing machine?"
    Answer: Keep fingers away from the needle area, never sew over pins, tie back long hair, switch off when threading or changing needles, sew at a controlled speed, and never pull fabric through - let the machine feed it. Always ask for help if you're unsure!

  • "What is oblique projection and why is it useful?"
    Answer: Oblique projection shows an object in 3D with the front face drawn true to size and the side going back at 45 degrees. It's useful because it's quick to draw, shows the product clearly, and helps communicate design ideas before making. The front view stays accurate while showing depth.
  • "What does ACCESSFMM stand for and why is it important?"
    Answer: ACCESSFMM stands for Aesthetics, Cost, Customer, Environment, Size, Safety, Function, Materials, and Manufacture. It's a framework for analyzing and evaluating designs, ensuring we consider all important factors when creating products. Good designers think about all these aspects, not just how something looks.
  • "How does a cam mechanism create movement?"
    Answer: A cam is a rotating disc with an irregular shape. As it rotates (rotary motion), it pushes against a follower, making it move up and down (linear motion). The cam's shape determines the follower's movement pattern. Mechanical toys use cams to create interesting animated motions!
  • "What safety procedures must I follow when using workshop tools?"
    Answer: Always: wear safety goggles, tie back long hair, secure loose clothing, use tools correctly for their intended purpose, keep work area tidy, focus on the task, ask for help if unsure, and never distract others. Workshop safety protects everyone and prevents accidents.
  • "Why is marking out accurately so important before cutting?"
    Answer: Accurate marking out ensures parts fit together properly and the finished product works as designed. Using a try square and sharp pencil to mark clear lines means you cut in the right place. "Measure twice, cut once" - mistakes in marking lead to wasted materials and time!
  • "What finishing techniques improve the quality of my product?"
    Answer: Good finishing includes: filling gaps and holes with wood filler, sanding smooth starting with rough sandpaper and finishing with fine, painting or varnishing evenly in thin coats, and ensuring all edges are smooth. Quality finishing transforms a good product into an excellent one!

  • "Why is food hygiene so important in the kitchen?"
    Answer: Food hygiene prevents harmful bacteria from growing and causing food poisoning. This means washing hands properly, keeping raw and cooked foods separate, cleaning surfaces regularly, cooking food to safe temperatures, and storing food correctly. Poor hygiene can make people seriously ill!
  • "What are the main food groups in the Eat Well Guide?"
    Answer: The five main food groups are: 1) Fruits and vegetables (1/3 of diet), 2) Starchy carbohydrates like bread, rice, pasta (1/3), 3) Protein from beans, fish, eggs, meat, 4) Dairy or alternatives for calcium, and 5) Small amounts of oils and spreads. Balance is key to healthy eating!
  • "What is enzymic browning and how can we prevent it?"
    Answer: Enzymic browning happens when cut fruits like apples or bananas react with oxygen in the air, turning brown due to enzymes. We can prevent it by: coating in lemon juice (acid stops enzymes), putting in water, or storing in airtight containers. It's food science in action!
  • "How do I safely use a knife for different cutting techniques?"
    Answer: Safe knife skills include: using a sharp knife on a stable chopping board, curling fingers to protect them (bridge and claw techniques), cutting away from your body, never catching a falling knife, and walking carefully with knife pointing down. Different cuts (dice, slice, julienne) need different techniques but always safety first!
  • "What makes a dish well-presented and appealing?"
    Answer: Good presentation includes: variety of colors, neat arrangement on the plate, appropriate portion sizes, garnishes that enhance flavor and appearance, clean plate edges, and food arranged with thought to height and balance. We eat with our eyes first - attractive food tastes better!
  • "What cooking methods are healthiest and why?"
    Answer: Healthier cooking methods include steaming, grilling, baking, and boiling because they need less fat than frying. They also preserve more vitamins and minerals. Grilling lets fat drip away, steaming keeps nutrients in, and these methods enhance natural flavors without adding lots of calories from oil.
