Frequently Asked Questions

What makes STM's Design & Technology curriculum unique?

Our curriculum combines traditional making skills with cutting-edge technology, including CAD/CAM systems and Photoshop. Students experience both Product Design and Food Technology, developing creative problem-solving skills while understanding the design process from conception to manufacture.

How does the curriculum prepare students for GCSE?

Year 7 introduces fundamental concepts like ACCESSFMM annotation criteria, technical drawing skills, and material understanding that form the foundation for GCSE Design & Technology. Students develop independence and analytical skills essential for higher-level study.

What practical skills will my child develop?

Students master both traditional craft skills (hand sewing, woodworking tools) and modern technology (CAD software, sewing machines, laser cutting). They learn marking out, cutting, drilling, joining, and finishing techniques alongside food preparation and presentation skills.

How is Food Technology integrated into the curriculum?

Food Technology runs alongside Product Design, covering nutrition science, food safety, and practical cooking skills. Students learn about the eat well guide, food groups, and healthy lifestyle choices while developing practical skills in food preparation and presentation.

What assessment methods are used?

Assessment combines practical making skills with design portfolio work. Students are assessed on technical accuracy, creative problem-solving, independence, and ability to evaluate their work against design criteria. Each term focuses on specific skill development and assessment.
