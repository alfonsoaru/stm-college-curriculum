History Year 7 Q&A

How does Year 7 History develop analytical skills?
Students learn to analyse historical sources, evaluate evidence, and make judgements about significance. They develop chronological understanding and learn to identify cause and consequence relationships, building essential foundations for historical thinking.
What time periods are covered in Year 7?
The curriculum spans from ancient Rome (BC/AD) through to the early Tudor period (c.1500), covering Roman Britain, Anglo-Saxon England, Norman Conquest, medieval life, Islamic empires, and the Crusades.
How are historical skills assessed?
Assessment includes source analysis with provenance, keyword knowledge, content understanding, and analytical writing. Students learn to evaluate factors, make judgements on significance, and explain cause and consequence relationships.
What cross-curricular connections are made?
History connects with Geography (volcanoes, population), Mathematics (negative numbers), CPSHE (democracy), Science (cell biology), and RE (Holy Land, Islam), providing broader educational context and reinforcing learning across subjects.
