Year 7 Historical Journey

Year 7 History at STM College takes students on an immersive journey through Medieval Britain, beginning with the dramatic events of 1066 and the Norman Conquest, then exploring life in medieval England through to 1500. Students develop crucial historical skills including chronological understanding, source analysis, causation, and the ability to construct well-argued historical narratives. Through studying castles, kings, battles, and everyday medieval life, students learn to think like historians, questioning evidence and building sophisticated interpretations of the past.

Key Historical Skills Development

Throughout Year 7, students progressively develop eight essential historical thinking skills that form the foundation for advanced historical analysis and interpretation.

üìÖ Chronological Understanding

Mastering timelines, dates, and the sequence of historical events from 1066 through medieval England

üìú Source Analysis

Evaluating primary and secondary sources, understanding provenance, and questioning historical evidence

üîç Cause & Consequence

Identifying why events happened and their short-term and long-term impacts on society

üîÑ Change & Continuity

Analyzing what changed and what stayed the same across different periods of medieval history

üëë Historical Significance

Evaluating the importance of events, people, and developments in shaping medieval England

üé≠ Multiple Perspectives

Understanding different viewpoints from Normans, Saxons, peasants, and nobles in medieval society

‚úçÔ∏è Historical Writing

Constructing clear, evidence-based arguments using appropriate historical terminology

üè∞ Historical Empathy

Understanding how people in the past thought, felt, and acted within their historical context

üçÇ Autumn Term - The Norman Conquest 1066

Weeks 1-7: Why Did William Win at Hastings?
  • Understand the succession crisis following Edward the Confessor's death in January 1066
  • Analyze the four claimants to the English throne: Harold Godwinson, William of Normandy, Harald Hardrada, and Edgar Atheling
  • Examine the Battle of Stamford Bridge and Harold's victory over the Vikings in September 1066
  • Evaluate William's preparation and the Norman invasion fleet crossing the English Channel
  • Analyze the Battle of Hastings tactics, including the shield wall and feigned retreat
  • Assess the reasons for William's victory: luck, leadership, tactics, and Harold's mistakes
  • Understand how the Bayeux Tapestry provides evidence about the Norman Conquest
  • Evaluate the reliability and bias of different historical sources about 1066

Key Vocabulary

People & Titles: Claimant, Successor, Oath, Witan, Earl, Duke
Military: Shield wall, Cavalry, Infantry, Feigned retreat, Tactics, Strategy
Evidence: Primary source, Secondary source, Bias, Propaganda, Bayeux Tapestry

Weeks 8-14: The Black Death & Its Consequences (1348-1350)
  • Understand how the Black Death arrived in England in 1348 and spread rapidly
  • Analyze medieval explanations for the plague: God's punishment, miasma theory, four humours
  • Examine medieval attempts to prevent and cure the plague: flagellants, plague doctors, quarantine
  • Evaluate the demographic impact: approximately one-third of England's population died
  • Analyze the economic consequences: labor shortages and rising wages for peasants
  • Understand the social changes: weakening of the Feudal System and peasant mobility
  • Examine the Peasants' Revolt of 1381 as a consequence of post-plague tensions
  • Assess the long-term significance of the Black Death in transforming medieval society
