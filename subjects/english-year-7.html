Frequently Asked Questions
Q: What reading level should my Year 7 child be at?
A: Year 7 students typically read at levels 4-6 on the National Curriculum scale. We assess each student individually and provide appropriate support or extension materials. Students should be comfortable with chapter books and beginning to analyze character motivations and themes.
Q: How much homework will there be each week?
A: Year 7 English homework averages 45-60 minutes per week, including reading assignments (20-30 mins), written tasks (15-20 mins), and vocabulary/spelling practice (10 mins). We believe in quality over quantity to build sustainable study habits.
Q: What literature texts will students study?
A: Year 7 students explore a mix of classic and contemporary texts including short story collections, poetry anthologies, and one class novel per term. Examples include works by Roald Dahl, Michael Morpurgo, and selections from "Stories from Different Cultures."
Q: How is speaking and listening assessed?
A: Students participate in regular discussions, presentations, and group work. Assessment focuses on clear communication, listening to others, and building confidence. We use peer feedback and self-reflection to support development in a supportive environment.
Q: What support is available for students who struggle with writing?
A: We offer differentiated writing frames, peer support systems, and additional support sessions. Students receive individual feedback on drafts, and we celebrate progress at every level. Our approach focuses on building confidence alongside technical skills.
Q: How can parents support their child's English learning at home?
A: Encourage daily reading for pleasure, discuss books and TV programs together, visit the library regularly, and show interest in their writing. Reading aloud together, even with older children, remains valuable for developing vocabulary and comprehension skills.
