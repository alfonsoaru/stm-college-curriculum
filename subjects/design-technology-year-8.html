  • "How does parametric design make CAD more efficient?" Parametric design uses variables and constraints so changing one dimension automatically updates related features - saves time and ensures consistency across design modifications.
  • "What's the difference between CAD and CAM in manufacturing?" CAD = Computer-Aided Design (creating the digital model). CAM = Computer-Aided Manufacture (using that model to control machines like laser cutters and CNC routers to make the actual product).
  • "How do series and parallel circuits affect LED brightness?" Series: LEDs share voltage - dimmer, one fails all fail. Parallel: Each LED gets full voltage - brighter, one fails others stay lit. Critical for reliable product design.
  • "Why is quality control testing essential for electronic products?" Ensures electrical safety (no shorts/overheating), confirms functionality (all features work), meets specifications, and prevents product failures that could harm users or damage reputation.
  • "What factors determine appropriate material selection for electronic housings?" Must be electrical insulator, heat resistant, durable, aesthetically appropriate, manufacturable with available tools, and cost-effective for production scale.
  • "How do sensors enhance product functionality in modern design?" Sensors enable automatic responses (light sensors for auto-dimming, motion sensors for activation) making products smarter, energy-efficient, and more user-friendly without manual control.

  • "How does batik create unique textile patterns?" Wax is applied to fabric before dyeing. Wax resists dye penetration creating patterns. Multiple layers of wax and dye create complex multi-color designs with characteristic crackle effects.
  • "Why use French seams instead of plain seams?" French seams enclose raw edges preventing fraying - gives professional finish, stronger construction, and cleaner appearance especially for reversible garments or delicate fabrics.
  • "What makes textiles 'smart' or 'technical'?" Smart textiles respond to environment (thermochromic changes color with heat, conductive thread conducts electricity). Technical textiles have enhanced properties like water resistance, flame retardancy, or UV protection.
  • "How does pattern adaptation enable custom fit and design?" Adjusting pattern dimensions, adding/removing design features, combining pattern elements allows personalization for body measurements, style preferences, and design innovation while maintaining proper construction.
  • "What sustainable practices are important in textile production?" Using organic/recycled materials, minimizing waste through efficient cutting, choosing low-impact dyes, designing for longevity, and considering end-of-life recyclability reduces environmental impact.
  • "Why is seam allowance consistency critical in quality construction?" Consistent seam allowance ensures proper fit (too much = too small, too little = too big), maintains design proportions, enables accurate pattern matching, and creates professional-quality finish.

I can now answer... ü§î

  • "Why is iterative design more effective than single-solution design?" Multiple cycles of design-prototype-test-refine reveal problems early, incorporate user feedback, explore alternatives, and progressively improve the solution leading to better final products than first-attempt designs.
  • "How does ACCESS FM help evaluate design quality?" ACCESS FM provides structured framework examining Aesthetics, Cost, Customer needs, Environment, Size, Safety, Function, Materials - ensures comprehensive evaluation covering all critical design aspects.
  • "What makes a design specification effective?" Clear, measurable criteria covering function, target market, constraints (size/cost/materials), aesthetics, safety requirements - specific enough to guide design but flexible enough to allow creative solutions.
  • "Why use orthographic projection instead of pictorial drawings for manufacturing?" Orthographic projections show true dimensions without perspective distortion, enable accurate measurements, reveal hidden details through sections, and provide precise manufacturing information that makers can follow exactly.
  • "How does prototyping reduce manufacturing costs and risks?" Prototypes identify design flaws before mass production, test functionality and user experience, allow inexpensive modifications, and prevent costly errors in final manufacturing runs.
  • "What role does client feedback play in user-centered design?" Client feedback reveals real needs vs. assumed needs, identifies usability issues designers miss, validates design decisions, and ensures final product actually solves the problem for its intended users.

I can now answer... ü§î

  • "How do gear trains change speed and force in mechanisms?" Gear ratio determines relationship: Large driving small = faster output/less force. Small driving large = slower output/more force. Essential for matching motor speed to application requirements.
  • "What is tolerance and why does it matter in manufacturing?" Tolerance = acceptable variation from ideal dimension (e.g., 50mm ¬±0.5mm). Tight tolerance = precise fit but expensive. Looser tolerance = cheaper but parts may not fit properly. Balance depends on function.
  • "Why use mortise and tenon or dovetail joints instead of simple joints?" These joints provide superior strength through mechanical interlock not just glue, resist pulling forces, create professional quality, and demonstrate advanced making skills essential for quality furniture/products.
  • "How does CNC manufacturing improve precision and consistency?" Computer control eliminates human error, repeats cuts identically thousands of times, achieves tighter tolerances than hand tools, enables complex curves/shapes, and allows design changes through software rather than retooling.
  • "What purpose do jigs and fixtures serve in batch production?" Jigs/fixtures hold work consistently in same position for each piece, ensuring identical results across batch, increasing production speed, improving safety, and maintaining quality standards throughout production run.
  • "How do surface finishing techniques affect product quality and durability?" Finishing protects materials from corrosion/wear, enhances appearance, provides smooth touchable surfaces, enables cleaning, and can add functional properties like water resistance or electrical insulation.

  • "How do macronutrients differ in function and dietary importance?" Proteins build/repair tissues (4 kcal/g). Carbohydrates provide energy (4 kcal/g). Fats provide concentrated energy and essential fatty acids (9 kcal/g). Balance all three for optimal health.
  • "What happens to protein during cooking (denaturation)?" Heat breaks bonds causing proteins to unravel and change structure - eggs solidify, meat becomes firm. Changes texture and digestibility but maintains nutritional value. Irreversible chemical process.
  • "Why does starch gelatinization matter in cooking?" Starch absorbs water and swells when heated, thickening sauces/gravies. Critical for pasta texture, bread structure, and sauce consistency. Understanding this controls cooking outcomes.
  • "How do dietary needs change across different life stages?" Children need growth nutrients (calcium/protein). Teens need extra calories for growth spurts. Adults need balanced maintenance. Elderly need nutrient-dense foods with fewer calories. Pregnancy/activity increases requirements.
  • "What role does sensory analysis play in food product development?" Testing appearance, aroma, taste, texture, and sound identifies problems, compares products, optimizes recipes, and ensures consumer acceptance before commercial production.
  • "Why is allergen awareness critical in food preparation?" Food allergies can cause severe reactions (anaphylaxis). Must prevent cross-contamination, clearly label allergens (14 major types), understand symptoms, and maintain strict hygiene protocols for consumer safety.

I can now answer... ü§î

  • "What makes an emulsified sauce stable and how is it achieved?" Emulsification combines oil and water (normally separate) using emulsifier (egg yolk, mustard). Slow addition while whisking creates stable mixture. Mayonnaise and hollandaise are classic examples.
  • "Why does gluten development matter in bread making?" Gluten (protein network from flour + water + kneading) traps CO2 from yeast, creating structure that rises and maintains shape. Without gluten development, bread would be dense and flat.
  • "How does lamination create flaky puff pastry?" Repeatedly folding butter into dough creates hundreds of thin layers. When baked, water in butter evaporates creating steam that separates layers - results in characteristic flaky, risen pastry.
  • "What preservation methods prevent bacterial growth and how?" Freezing halts bacterial growth. Drying removes water bacteria need. Pickling uses acid/salt. Vacuum packing removes oxygen. Each method creates environment where bacteria cannot survive or reproduce.
  • "Why is mise en place essential for efficient food production?" Preparing all ingredients before cooking (mise en place = 'everything in place') ensures smooth workflow, prevents missing ingredients mid-recipe, manages timing correctly, and produces professional results.
  • "How does understanding cooking science improve culinary outcomes?" Knowing why techniques work (not just how) enables problem-solving, recipe adaptation, creative innovation, and consistent results - transforms cooking from following instructions to skilled application of scientific principles.
