üî¨ Biology

"What's the difference between sexual and asexual reproduction?"

üí°

Sexual: 2 parents, gametes, variation. Asexual: 1 parent, no gametes, identical clones.

"How do genes determine characteristics?"

üí°

Genes (in DNA on chromosomes) carry instructions for characteristics. You inherit genes from both parents - that's why you look similar to family.

"Why is genetic variation important for species survival?"

üí°

Variation means some individuals survive environmental changes (disease, climate). Without variation, whole species could die out.

üß™ Chemistry

"How are elements arranged in the periodic table?"

üí°

By atomic number (protons). Groups (columns) have similar properties. Periods (rows) have same electron shells.

"What's inside an atom?"

üí°

Nucleus (centre): protons (+) and neutrons (neutral). Electrons (-) orbit around the nucleus in shells.

"How do atoms bond to form compounds?"

üí°

Atoms want full outer electron shells. Ionic: transfer electrons. Covalent: share electrons. Both create stable compounds.

‚ö° Physics

"What's the difference between series and parallel circuits?"

üí°

Series: one path, same current, shared voltage. Parallel: multiple paths, current splits, full voltage each branch.

"How do I calculate electrical power?"

üí°

Power = Voltage √ó Current (P = V √ó I). Measured in watts. Higher power = more energy per second.

"What creates a magnetic field around a wire?"

üí°

Electric current flowing through a wire creates a magnetic field. Coiling the wire makes an electromagnet - stronger field, can be switched on/off.

üî¨ Biology

"How do food chains show energy transfer in ecosystems?"

üí°

Energy flows Sun‚ÜíProducer‚ÜíConsumer. Only 10% transfers each level, 90% lost as heat. This limits food chain length.

"What factors affect population size in ecosystems?"

üí°

Food availability, predation, disease, space/territory, competition, climate, human activity. Balance determines if population grows or shrinks.

"How do organisms adapt to their environment?"

üí°

Structural (body features), behavioural (actions), physiological (internal processes). Adaptations help survival and reproduction.

üß™ Chemistry

"How can I tell if a solution is acidic, neutral, or alkaline?"

üí°

Use pH indicators: 0-6 acidic (red), 7 neutral (green), 8-14 alkaline (blue/purple). Universal indicator most accurate.

"What happens when you increase reaction temperature?"

üí°

Higher temperature = faster reaction. Particles move faster, collide more often and with more energy.

"How do you write a balanced chemical equation?"

üí°

Reactants ‚Üí Products. Same number of each type of atom on both sides. Use numbers in front of formulas to balance.

‚ö° Physics

"What determines the pitch and loudness of a sound wave?"

üí°

Frequency determines pitch (high frequency = high pitch). Amplitude determines loudness (big amplitude = loud sound).

"Why does light bend when it goes through glass?"

üí°

Refraction: light changes speed when entering different materials. Speed change causes direction change. This is how lenses and prisms work.

"What are the different types of electromagnetic waves?"

üí°

Radio waves, microwaves, infrared, visible light, ultraviolet, X-rays, gamma rays. All travel at speed of light, different frequencies/energies.
