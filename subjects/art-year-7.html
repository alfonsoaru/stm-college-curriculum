Terms 1a & 1b - Line, Shape, Colour, Tone & Texture

Term 1a - Formal Elements

Line, Shape & Colour

Introduction to the fundamental building blocks of art. Students explore different types of lines, organic and geometric shapes, and the colour wheel including primary, secondary, and complementary colours.

Key Skills: Line variety (wiggling, sensitive, bold), shape identification, colour theory, visual vocabulary development

Term 1b - Formal Elements

Tone & Texture

Advanced exploration of visual elements focusing on light, mid and dark tones, gradient blending, and both implied and actual textures through various mark-making techniques.

Key Skills: Tone gradients, texture creation (scribbling, stippling, cross-hatching), frottage techniques, critical analysis

Term 2a - Pattern & Composition

Pattern & Composition

Understanding how patterns are created through repetition, rotation, and reflection. Introduction to composition principles including space, focal points, and visual balance.

Key Skills: Pattern creation (repetition, half-drop repeat), composition understanding, tessellation, M.C. Escher studies

Connections: Mathematics (geometry), Science (colour theory), Geography (contour lines), Languages (cultural artists)

Composition Development

Key Art Vocabulary

Students develop essential artistic vocabulary throughout Year 7, building the language needed to discuss and analyze artworks:

Line

Wiggling, sensitive, faint, bold, contour, expressive, structural, gestural

Shape & Form

Organic, geometric, 2D, 3D, positive space, negative space, silhouette

Colour

Primary, secondary, tertiary, complementary, warm, cold, analogous, hue, saturation

Tone

Light, mid-tone, dark, gradient, blending, scale, contrast, highlight, shadow

Texture

Implied, actual, mark-making, scribbling, stippling, cross-hatching, frottage, surface quality

Pattern & Composition

Repetition, rotation, reflection, half-drop repeat, focal point, balance, space, unity

Assessment & Portfolio Development

Year 7 Art assessment is portfolio-based, tracking progress from baseline to final exhibition pieces. Students complete critical analysis of artists' work, develop practical skills, and demonstrate understanding of formal elements through creative projects.

Cross-Curricular Connections

Art education at STM College connects meaningfully with other subjects, enriching learning across the curriculum:

Mathematics

Geometric shapes, tessellation, rotation, reflection, proportion, and symmetry through artists like M.C. Escher.

Science

Colour theory, light sources, natural patterns, biodiversity studies, and anatomy through observational drawing.

Languages

Cultural connections through artists like Henri Matisse (French) and Max Ernst (German), expanding cultural awareness.

Geography & History

Contour lines, traditional and contemporary art movements, and understanding art in historical and cultural contexts.
