Computer Science Year 7 Q&A

What programming languages and tools will students learn?
Students use Code.org for visual programming, KODU for 3D game creation, and microbit block programming. This progression builds from visual to text-based programming concepts while maintaining engagement through practical projects.
How is e-safety integrated throughout the curriculum?
E-safety is foundational, covering responsible internet use, social media safety, digital footprints, data protection, and cyberbullying prevention. Students learn through case studies including safe Snapchat use and identifying fake news.
What computational thinking skills are developed?
Students develop pattern recognition, decomposition, abstraction, and algorithmic thinking through practical programming projects. They learn sequencing, selection, and iteration while solving real-world problems.
How do projects connect to real-world applications?
Projects include theme park data management using spreadsheets, game design for target audiences, and microbit programming for interactive devices. Students see how computing concepts apply to entertainment, business, and technology industries.
